## Getting Started

### What You Need

- **A Garmin device** with GPS and heart rate tracking
- **A completed 5K run** recorded on your device
- **Internet connection** to access the analyzer
- **10 minutes** to review your comprehensive analysis

### Supported File Formats

The analyzer accepts three standard Garmin export formats:

| Format | Description | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **FIT** | Native Garmin format | Most detailed metrics including advanced running dynamics |
| **TCX** | Training Center XML | Compatible with most running platforms |
| **GPX** | GPS Exchange format | Basic GPS and heart rate data |

**Recommendation**: Always use FIT files when possible for the most comprehensive analysis.

## Uploading Your Data

### Step 1: Export from Garmin Connect

1. **Log in to Garmin Connect** (web or mobile app)
2. **Navigate to your 5K activity**
3. **Click the gear icon** (Settings/Options)
4. **Select "Export Original"** to download the FIT file
   - Alternative: "Export to TCX" or "Export to GPX"
5. **Save the file** to your computer

### Step 2: Upload to Analyzer

#### Method A: Drag and Drop (Recommended)
1. Open the analyzer in your web browser
2. Drag your exported file directly onto the upload area
3. Release to begin automatic analysis

#### Method B: File Browser
1. Click the **"Choose File"** button
2. Navigate to your downloaded Garmin file
3. Select the file and click "Open"
4. Analysis begins automatically

#### Method C: Try Sample Data
1. Click **"Try Sample Data"** to see the analyzer in action
2. Review a pre-loaded analysis to understand the features
3. Upload your own file when ready

### Performance Metrics Dashboard

#### Core Metrics

**Total Time**: Your finish time in HH:MM:SS format
- Compared against typical times for your profile

**Average Pace**: Per-kilometer pace throughout the race
- Displayed as MM:SS per km
- Consistency indicated by variance

**Distance**: Verified race distance
- GPS accuracy assessment
- Elevation gain/loss if available

#### Physiological Metrics

**Average Heart Rate**: Mean HR during the race
- Compared to age-predicted max (220 - age)
- Zone classification provided

**Max Heart Rate**: Peak HR reached
- Indicates maximum effort point
- Should be sustainable for 5K distance

**Heart Rate Zones**: Percentage of time in each zone
- Zone 1 (50-60%): Recovery
- Zone 2 (60-70%): Aerobic base
- Zone 3 (70-80%): Tempo
- Zone 4 (80-90%): Threshold
- Zone 5 (90-100%): Maximum

#### Running Form Metrics

**Cadence (steps/minute)**:
- Optimal range: 170-180 spm
- Lower cadence = higher impact forces
- Higher cadence = increased efficiency

**Stride Length**: Distance covered per step
- Calculated from pace and cadence
- Optimal varies by height

**Vertical Oscillation**: Up/down bounce
- Lower is generally better
- Optimal: <10cm
- Measured by advanced Garmin devices only

**Ground Contact Time**: Time foot is on ground
- Lower indicates better efficiency
- Optimal: <250ms
- Requires compatible device

### Form Corrections

Specific drills to address identified weaknesses:

#### Cadence Improvement (if <170 spm)
**Problem**: Low cadence increases impact and injury risk

**Drills**:
1. **Metronome Training**
   - Use metronome app at 170 bpm
   - 4 x 200m matching cadence
   - 2-3 times per week
   - Expected timeline: 4-6 weeks to habituate

2. **Downhill Strides**
   - Find gentle downhill (3-5% grade)
   - 6 x 100m focusing on quick turnover
   - Let gravity assist faster cadence
   - 1-2 times per week

3. **High Knees Drill**
   - 4 x 30 seconds
   - Focus on rapid leg turnover
   - Before easy runs
   - Builds neuromuscular patterns

#### Pacing Consistency (if high variance)
**Problem**: Uneven pacing wastes energy

**Strategies**:
1. **GPS Watch Discipline**
   - Set pace alerts (Â±5 seconds per km)
   - Check watch every 250m
   - Practice in training runs

2. **Even Effort Intervals**
   - Run intervals by feel, not pace
   - Check pace after completion
   - Learn to self-regulate

3. **Progressive Long Runs**
   - Start deliberately slow
   - Gradually increase pace
   - Builds pacing awareness

#### Heart Rate Management (if too high)
**Problem**: Unsustainable effort level

**Training**:
1. **80/20 Training**
   - 80% of runs at easy HR (Zone 2)
   - 20% at moderate-hard (Zone 3-4)
   - Builds aerobic efficiency

2. **Heart Rate Ceiling Runs**
   - Set HR limit (e.g., 150 bpm)
   - Maintain pace below that HR
   - Teaches effort regulation

3. **Recovery Emphasis**
   - Ensure 48 hours between hard sessions
   - Add extra easy day if needed
   - Prevents chronic fatigue

## Interpreting Metrics

### Pace Analysis

**What It Means**:
- Pace = Time to cover 1 kilometer
- Lower pace number = faster running
- Example: 5:00/km is faster than 6:00/km

**Pace Variance**:
- **Low variance (<5%)**: Excellent pacing consistency
- **Medium variance (5-10%)**: Acceptable pacing
- **High variance (>10%)**: Energy wasted, room for improvement

**Pacing Strategies**:

1. **Even Split**: Same pace throughout
   - Easiest to execute
   - Good for beginners
   - Reduces risk of early fatigue

2. **Negative Split**: Faster second half
   - Optimal for performance
   - Requires experience and restraint
   - Maximizes race result

3. **Positive Split**: Slower second half
   - Common in aggressive starts
   - Indicates poor pacing strategy
   - Work on starting conservatively

### Cadence Analysis

**Optimal Range**: 170-180 steps per minute

**Why It Matters**:
- Higher cadence = shorter ground contact = less impact
- Reduces injury risk to knees, hips, feet
- Improves running economy

**Your Cadence**:
- **<160 spm**: Priority improvement area
- **160-170 spm**: Work on increasing
- **170-180 spm**: Optimal range
- **>180 spm**: May indicate overstriding compensation

**Adjustments**:
- Increase cadence by 5% every 2-3 weeks
- Don't force dramatic changes
- Focus on quick, light foot strikes

### Heart Rate Analysis

**Understanding Zones**:

| Zone | % Max HR | Feel | Purpose | Time in Zone |
|------|----------|------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | 50-60% | Very easy | Recovery | Minimal in race |
| 2 | 60-70% | Easy | Aerobic base | Should be 0 in 5K |
| 3 | 70-80% | Moderate | Tempo training | Some in early km |
| 4 | 80-90% | Hard | Threshold | Majority of race |
| 5 | 90-100% | Maximum | All-out | Final km or less |

**Ideal 5K Heart Rate**:
- Should average 85-92% of max HR
- Start around 80-85% (Zone 3-4)
- Build to 90-95% (Zone 5) in final km
- If average is <85%: Left effort on the table
- If average is >95%: Likely went out too hard

**Max Heart Rate Estimation**:
- Common formula: 220 - age
- Individual variation: Â±10-20 beats
- Better to test with all-out 5K effort

### Running Form Metrics

#### Vertical Oscillation
**What It Is**: Vertical bounce in running gait
**Optimal**: <10 cm
**High oscillation**: Wasted energy going up instead of forward
**Causes**: Overstriding, weak core, heel striking
**Improvements**: Cadence drills, core work, midfoot striking

#### Ground Contact Time (GCT)
**What It Is**: Time foot is on ground each step
**Optimal**: 200-250 ms
**High GCT**: Indicates heavy landing
**Low GCT**: Suggests efficient toe-off
**Note**: Varies by pace (slower = longer GCT)

## Troubleshooting

### Upload Issues

**Problem**: File won't upload
- **Check format**: Must be .fit, .tcx, or .gpx
- **Check size**: Must be under 10MB
- **Try different browser**: Chrome/Firefox recommended
- **Check internet**: Stable connection required

**Problem**: "Invalid file" error
- **Re-export from Garmin**: File may be corrupted
- **Try different format**: Export as TCX if FIT fails
- **Check file**: Make sure it's a 5K race, not other activity

**Problem**: Upload stalls at "Analyzing..."
- **Refresh page**: Start over
- **Try sample data**: Verify system is working
- **Check file size**: Very large files take longer
- **Contact support**: If issue persists

### Analysis Issues

**Problem**: Missing metrics in results
- **Device limitations**: Older Garmin devices lack advanced metrics
- **File format**: TCX/GPX have fewer metrics than FIT
- **Expected behavior**: Analysis adapts to available data
- **Solution**: Use FIT files from newer devices for complete analysis

**Problem**: Metrics seem inaccurate
- **GPS issues**: Check if GPS signal was good during run
- **Device placement**: Heart rate monitor must contact skin
- **Calibration**: Footpod needs calibration if used
- **Verify source**: Check data in Garmin Connect first

**Problem**: Recommendations don't make sense
- **Profile data**: Ensure age/gender entered correctly (if prompted)
- **Race distance**: Must be approximately 5K distance
- **Unusual run**: System designed for race efforts, not easy runs
- **Contact support**: Share specific concerns

### Training Plan Issues

**Problem**: Plan seems too hard
- **Start easier**: Follow "easy" day guidelines strictly
- **Reduce volume**: Cut weekly mileage by 20%
- **More rest**: Add extra recovery day
- **Be patient**: Fitness takes time

**Problem**: Plan seems too easy
- **Verify profile**: Make sure fitness level is accurate
- **Follow exactly**: Don't skip warmup/cooldown
- **Increase intensity**: Hit prescribed paces precisely
- **Consider goal**: Focus on consistency first

**Problem**: Can't complete workouts
- **Overtraining signs**: Excess fatigue, elevated resting HR
- **Need recovery**: Take 3-5 easier days
- **Reduce volume**: Scale back 20-30%
- **See coach**: Consider professional guidance

## FAQ

### General Questions

**Q: How accurate is the analysis?**
A: Analysis uses research-backed algorithms with 85-98% confidence scores. Individual variation exists, but recommendations are based on sports science consensus.

**Q: Can I use this for other distances?**
A: System is optimized for 5K but works for 3K-10K. Different distances may have less accurate recommendations.

**Q: Do I need a premium Garmin device?**
A: No. Basic GPS + HR is sufficient. Advanced metrics (vertical oscillation, ground contact time) require Running Dynamics devices.

**Q: How often should I analyze runs?**
A: Analyze race efforts or hard 5K time trials. Every 2-4 weeks is typical. Don't analyze every training run.

**Q: Is my data private?**
A: Currently, uploaded data is processed and not stored. Check privacy policy for current data handling practices.

### Training Questions

**Q: Can beginners use this?**
A: Yes! Analysis and recommendations adapt to all fitness levels from beginner to advanced.

**Q: What if I can't run 7 days a week?**
A: Adjust plan to your schedule. 4-5 days is fine. Quality over quantity. Include rest days.

**Q: Should I follow the plan exactly?**
A: Use as a guide, not law. Listen to your body. Adjust based on how you feel and respond.

**Q: When will I see improvements?**
A: Noticeable changes in 3-4 weeks. Significant improvements in 8-12 weeks with consistent training.

**Q: What if I get injured?**
A: Stop running immediately. Rest, ice, elevate. See medical professional. Don't rush back.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Why FIT files over TCX/GPX?**
A: FIT is Garmin's native format with most complete data including all sensors and advanced metrics.

**Q: Can I analyze treadmill runs?**
A: Yes, if device recorded it. GPS accuracy may be lower. Footpod recommended for treadmills.

**Q: What about outdoor track runs?**
A: Yes! Track provides accurate distance. GPS can be less accurate on track curves.

**Q: My watch says 5.1K, is that okay?**
A: Yes. GPS variance is normal. Within 5.2K is acceptable for 5K analysis.

**Q: Why are my paces in min/km not min/mile?**
A: Currently displays kilometers. Future update may add unit preferences.
